Idaho 1099-C Cancellation of Debt [2026]: IRC 108, Form 982, and ID State Tax

State-specific rules, federal court data, and practical guidance for Idaho residents.

What Is a 1099-C in Idaho?

When a creditor cancels $600 or more of your debt, they are required by 26 U.S.C. 6050P to issue IRS Form 1099-C "Cancellation of Debt." Cancelled debt is generally treated as ordinary income under IRC 61(a)(11) - unless you qualify for an exclusion.

Idaho follows federal guidance, but the state tax treatment depends on whether Idaho's income tax code conforms to IRC 108.

For most consumer credit card forgiveness / debt settlement outside bankruptcy, the insolvency exclusion is the workhorse. You calculate your total liabilities and total FMV of assets the day before cancellation and exclude up to the insolvency amount.

Idaho State Income Tax Treatment of 1099-C Income

Idaho posture: Static conform

What this means in practice:

  • If Idaho conforms to IRC 108 (rolling or static), your bankruptcy or insolvency exclusion on the federal return flows through to your Idaho return.
  • If Idaho has no income tax, you owe no state tax on 1099-C income regardless of federal outcome.
  • If Idaho is decoupled or static-conforming to an older IRC, you may need to add back or adjust on your Idaho return. Idaho DOR published guidance is the source of truth.

Form 982 Filing Guidance for Idaho Taxpayers

Form 982 ("Reduction of Tax Attributes Due to Discharge of Indebtedness") is how you claim the exclusion on your federal return. Key boxes:

  • Box 1a - Discharge in a Title 11 case (bankruptcy). Always use this box if the debt was discharged in your Idaho bankruptcy case.
  • Box 1b - Insolvency. Attach a worksheet showing total liabilities and total FMV of assets immediately before the cancellation.
  • Box 1e - Qualified principal residence indebtedness (QPRI).
  • Line 10a-10b - Reduction of basis in property (complex; often applies to QPRI).

For most Idaho consumer-debt settlements, the 1b insolvency worksheet is the document that does the work. Preserve proof of liabilities and asset values as of the cancellation date.

1099-C Common Pitfalls in Idaho

  1. Received a 1099-C for old charged-off debt. Sometimes creditors issue 1099-Cs years after charge-off. The IRS "identifiable event" rules (Treas. Reg. 1.6050P-1) list eight triggering events. You may be able to contest the issuance if no true "discharge" occurred.
  2. Zombie 1099-C. Debt buyer issues 1099-C then later tries to collect. IRS has informally indicated creditors cannot treat a debt as both cancelled (1099-C) and collectible, but courts are split.
  3. Settled-debt 1099-C after Idaho bankruptcy filed. If the debt was listed in your bankruptcy schedules, the Title 11 exclusion applies; creditor may still issue the 1099-C in error.
  4. Spouse liability. Joint account 1099-C allocation can affect the insolvency worksheet; document separately-titled assets.

When Idaho Bankruptcy Beats Settlement for Tax Reasons

Example: Idaho household with $42,120 in unsecured debt. Settlement at 50% = roughly $21,060 forgiven = potential ordinary income of the same amount. At 22% federal + Idaho bracket, that is real money.

If the household is insolvent, the insolvency exclusion may cover most or all of it. But if the household has meaningful equity (home, retirement), insolvency exclusion may be limited - and the 1099-C tax exposure can exceed what bankruptcy legal fees would have cost.

The Title 11 exclusion is unconditional: any debt discharged in a Idaho Chapter 7 or 13 case is excluded regardless of solvency.
